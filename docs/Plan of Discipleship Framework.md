Plan of Discipleship Framework: The Four Elements of Discipleship

Dr. Gifford's "Plan of Discipleship" has four distinct categories that must be customized based on the child's **Age Bracket** (0-3, 3-6, 7-12, 13-17). The goal remains constant: to raise children who glorify God and possess the character and skills to live responsibly in His world.

* **1. Character (Christ-likeness):**
  * **Definition:** Moral and spiritual attributes that reflect Jesus (e.g., kindness, self-control, truthfulness).
  * **Strategy:** Pick 2–3 specific traits per year. Don’t try to fix everything at once. Focus on what is arguably lacking or developmentally necessary.
  * **Biblical Basis:** 2 Peter 1:5-7 (Supplement your faith with virtue...).
* **2. Competencies (Life Skills):**
  * **Definition:** Practical skills required for responsible living (professional/personal).
  * **Strategy:** These must be age-appropriate.
    * *Professional:* School grades, work ethic, handling money.
    * *Personal:* Hygiene, social skills (eye contact, handshakes), devotional habits.
  * **Biblical Basis:** 2 Thessalonians 3:10-12 (The value of work and quiet living); 1 Thessalonians 4:11.
* **3. Blessings (The Harvest of Obedience):**
  * **Definition:** Positive outcomes for honoring God and parents.
  * **Types:**
    * *Practical:* Screen time, ice cream, special outings, monetary rewards (for older kids).
    * *Spiritual:* Affirmation of pleasing the Lord, peace in the home, joy.
  * **Biblical Basis:** Ephesians 6:2-3 ("...that it may go well with you").
* **4. Consequences (The Harvest of Disobedience):**
  * **Definition:** Negative outcomes for dishonoring God and parents.
  * **Strategy:** Must be age-appropriate (e.g., no spanking for a 17-year-old; no taking away car keys from a 4-year-old).
  * **Biblical Basis:** Galatians 6:7 (Reaping what you sow); Proverbs 22:15.
